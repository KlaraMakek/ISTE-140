Netscape

Netscape Communications' navigator, often known as a browser, was its most well-known product. In the 1990s, it was one of the two most popular and frequently used web browsers. It was at odds with the internet's all-time great, Internet Explorer. Netscape was the first browser to "introduce" graphics in order to make the web more user-friendly. This is why Netscape is such an important part of the history of the internet. They not only established a new standard, which is still widely used today, but they also lost "the fight," which is ironic. According to one MND writer, "despite Netscape's technical advantages and initial supremacy, Internet Explorer swiftly exceeded Netscape in market penetration by the late 1990s." [1]
