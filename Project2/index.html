The internet

Various computer networks all across the world may now link thanks to the Internet, a system design that has transformed communications and techniques of trade. The Internet, often known as a "network of networks," first appeared in the United States in the 1970s, but it wasn't until the early 1990s that it was made widely accessible. It was predicted that 4.5 billion people, or more than half of the world's population, will have access to the Internet by 2020.[2]

Every person who connects to one of its constituent networks has access to the Internet's vast and all-encompassing capabilities, which may be utilized for virtually any purpose that relies on information. It provides human contact through social media, e-mail, "chat rooms," newsgroups, and audio and video transmission and enables teamwork across several locations. Many programs, like the World Wide Web, can use it to access digital information. A vast and expanding number of "e-businesses" that conduct the majority of their sales and services online, including subsidiaries of conventional "brick-and-mortar" enterprises, have found success on the Internet.[2]

World Wide Web

The Web, often known as the World Wide Web (WWW), is the top Internet information retrieval service (the worldwide computer network). Hypertext or hypermedia links, often known as hyperlinks, are electronic connections that connect similar bits of information to offer a user simple access to them. The Web provides users with access to a wide variety of documents that are connected to one another through these links. The user can visit other papers that contain more information on a word or phrase by selecting it from text that contains hypertext. Links to videos, animations, sounds, and photos can be found in hypermedia documents.[3]

The Web operates within the Internet's basic client-server format; servers are computer programs that store and transmit documents to other computers on the network when asked to, while clients are programs that request documents from a server as the user asks for them. Browser software allows users to view the retrieved documents.[3]
